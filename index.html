The Invisible
Crisis

Microplastics (MPs) are captured in wastewater treatment plant sludge at over 90% of the particles entering the plant. Yet current treatment methods merely concentrate these pollutants rather than eliminating them.

When contaminated sludge is land-applied or incinerated, microplastics spread into soils and food chains, threatening ecosystem health and crop safety.
